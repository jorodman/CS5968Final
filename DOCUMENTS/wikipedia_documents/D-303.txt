[2] It is also a surname. The name is attested from at least the 9th century. West Germanic Bernhard is composed from the two elements bern "bear" and hard "brave, hardy". [3] Its native Old English reflex was Beornheard , which was replaced by the French form Bernard that was brought to England after the Norman Conquest . The name Bernhard was notably popular among Old Frisian speakers. [4] Its wider use was popularized due to Saint Bernhard of Clairvaux (canonized in 1174). Bernard is the second most common surname in France .
